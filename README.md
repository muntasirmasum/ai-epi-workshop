## ğŸ“‹ Workshop Overview

This 75-minute demonstration-style workshop showcases how AI assistants can enhance statistical education in graduate epidemiology courses. The session features live demonstrations of Claude AI integrated with RStudio for teaching regression analysis methods.

**Format:** Primarily demonstration with optional follow-along resources
**Audience:** Mixed backgrounds (students, faculty, researchers, public health professionals)
**Prerequisites:** None required - no prior experience with R or AI tools necessary

---

## ğŸ“ Learning Objectives

By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. **Understand AI-Enhanced Pedagogy:** Recognize how AI assistants can scaffold learning in quantitative methods courses while maintaining academic rigor
2. **Observe Practical Workflows:** See real-time demonstrations of Claude integrated with RStudio for regression analysis
3. **Design AI-Enhanced Assignments:** Develop strategies for creating assignments that leverage AI tools appropriately
4. **Facilitate Collaborative Research:** Explore approaches for organizing student research projects with AI assistance

## ğŸ’¡ Workshop Philosophy

This workshop demonstrates an approach to AI integration that:
- **Maintains Academic Rigor:** AI as a teaching assistant, not a replacement for learning
- **Promotes Active Learning:** Students engage with concepts while AI scaffolds understanding
- **Emphasizes Transparency:** Clear guidelines on appropriate AI use in coursework
- **Builds Skills:** Focus on interpretation and critical thinking, not just computation
